The old lighthouse keeper, Elias, polished the glass one last time. Below, the sea roared like a hungry beast, clawing at the jagged rocks. For forty years, he had been the silent guardian of the coast, a beacon in the dark.

As he turned the heavy brass crank, the light cut through the fog, sweeping across the waves. In the distance, a faint horn blared in gratitude. Elias smiled, his weathered face creasing. He wasn't just tending a lamp; he was holding back the night.
